# Schizophrenia Polygenic Risk Analysis Project

## Introduction
This project focuses on the analysis of polygenic risk scores related to schizophrenia. The goal is to understand the genetic components that contribute to the risk of developing this psychiatric disorder.

## Objectives
1. To identify polygenic risk factors associated with schizophrenia.
2. To analyze the impact of these genetic factors on the overall risk of developing the disorder.
3. To provide insights into potential interventions based on genetic predispositions.

## Methodology
- **Data Collection**: Gather genomic data from various studies and databases.
- **Statistical Analysis**: Use advanced statistical methods to calculate polygenic risk scores.
- **Interpretation**: Correlate risk scores with clinical outcomes and environmental factors.

## Results
The project will report findings on the identified genetic variants, their respective contributions to risk scores and any actionable insights derived from these results.

## Conclusion
Understanding the genetic underpinnings of schizophrenia can enhance our comprehension of the disorder and promote more targeted therapeutic approaches.

## Future Work
This initiative may lead to further studies and collaborations aimed at deepening the understanding of schizophrenia and improving care for those affected by it.
