Primary Research Objectives ;,
1. Establish a Genomic Foundation for Psychiatric Disease Risk Modeling
     Integrate large-scale GWAS summary statistics from the Psychiatric Genomics Consortium with population genotype data from the 1000 Genomes Project
     Harmonize genetic variants across heterogeneous datasets through quality control, allele matching, and strand correction
     Create a reproducible computational pipeline for calculating individual-level polygenic risk scores (PRS) for schizophrenia

2. Quantify Aggregate Genetic Liability to Schizophrenia
    Calculate polygenic risk scores that capture genome-wide association signals
    Characterize the distribution of genetic risk across diverse global populations from the 1000 Genomes Project

3. Evaluate Population-Level Genetic Risk Stratification
    Assess variation in schizophrenia genetic risk across different continental ancestry groups
    Identify how polygenic risk is distributed in the population (e.g., what percentage of individuals fall into high/medium/low risk categories)
    Analyze the relationship between genetic architecture and population ancestry

4. Establish Infrastructure for Multi-Modal Integration
Build a robust, modular pipeline that can serve as a foundation for integrating additional data modalities
Create standardized data formats and quality control benchmarks that will enable seamless incorporation of:
Transcriptomic data (gene expression patterns in relevant tissues)
Neuroimaging data (structural and functional brain measurements)
Demonstrate how polygenic risk can be combined with molecular and imaging phenotypes to improve disease prediction
Secondary/Supporting Objectives

5. Implement Rigorous Quality Control Standards
Perform variant-level QC (removing multi-allelic variants, low-quality calls)
Implement individual-level QC (handling missing data, population structure analysis)
Document all filtering steps and retention metrics for reproducibility

6. Create a Reproducible Research Workflow
Develop a transparent, well-documented pipeline that others can adapt
Establish version control and checkpoint systems for large genomic datasets
Enable scalability to other psychiatric conditions (bipolar disorder, depression, etc.)
